Master Mobile UX Course Notes

Rewire your brain

If you design for the smallest, least powerful device first, then you will focus on the content, and your core functionality. That leads to simple, beautiful apps. If you do it the other way around, it'll be like trying to put a marshmallow into a piggy bankd, which is neither simple, nor beautiful.
-Joel Marsh

Do mobile first because people will be way more likely to be using the mobile device. It's easier to grow things than shrink them.

Responsive != mobile first

Understand Users

It's really hard to design products by focus groups. a lot of times people don't know what they want until you show it to them.
-Steve Jobs

People don't know what they want. Be there to show them where to go and what to do. Be useful, help them accomplish their purpose. Be quick. Nobody has an attention span these days. Make interactions smooth and seamless. Delete extra steps they don't need.

Reachability - people rely on their thumbs to reach things on their phone. Make important things easy to reach.

Speed - even .5 second can make a huge difference. And a lot of people don't have fast internet.

Assets - Use svg and webfonts when possible. Optimize and minify whenever possible.

Strategic Menu Design

Be empathetic to users. They are not always good at figuring out unintuitive software.

Why is menu always all the way at the top where you can't even reach it?? Put it down at the bottom where the thumb can get it.

Problems with the hamburger menu - not everyone knows what that is! The word "menu" with the border makes a lot more sense.

Mobile Friendly Forms

Problems with forms: Too much uncessary information that make the form too long.

Things to try: ditch the extra labels. Make inputs taller for thumbs.

Float labels are cool.

Note to self: learn how to make a float label

Make bigger checkboxes

Ask the user what they want. Then only make them fill out relevant info.

Comments: make placeholder specific to what you want

paginate forms. And use a progress indicator.

Also enable auto complete:See here for more info.

Passwords - masking can make it frustrating. Have option to show or hide password.

Credit cards - Auto spacing is cool for easy entry. Maybe even put everything in the same input.

Choose which keyboard to use on mobile. If it's just numbers, the letter keyboard will be cumbersome. Helpful article

Make phonenumbers callable by putting them in an anchor tag. No more copy/paste!

Better Mobile Advertising

Research to understand the way people use mobile devices.

Too many ads are very very annoying on mobile.

How to make mobile ads better:

Make them very small and simple. Not taking up the whole screen.

Cool idea: put a window to show a full screen add between the content.

Just don't use pop ups. They came for content, not ads.
